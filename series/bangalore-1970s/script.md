# "Time-Traveler Vlog: A Walk Down MG Road, 1970s Bangalore"
### Selfie-vlog AI video — narrated by you (JK)

**Concept:** One continuous walk down MG Road in mid-1970s Bangalore. You — a present-day person in modern clothes — stroll the length of the boulevard, talking to camera about the shops, the ice cream parlor, the theatres, the (almost non-existent) traffic, and the easy pace of life back then.

**Format:** first-person selfie-vlog, ~75 seconds, nine 8-second clips.
**Recommended tool:** Google **Veo 3** (generates the in-scene spoken narration + lip-sync natively). Sora 2 or Kling also work, but you'd add voice separately.
**Aspect ratio:** 9:16 vertical (Reels/Shorts/TikTok). Use 16:9 for YouTube landscape.

---

## How to make "you" the narrator (do this first)

Veo/Sora can't invent your face, so you anchor your likeness once and reuse it:

1. **Character reference:** Upload one clear, front-facing photo of yourself as the character/reference image in Veo. In each prompt, refer to yourself as **"the narrator (see reference image)."**
2. **Consistency line — keep it identical in every prompt** (edit to match you):
   > *"The narrator is a man in his [30s/40s], [medium-brown skin], short black hair, [light stubble], wearing a modern charcoal quarter-zip and jeans, holding the camera at arm's length (selfie-vlog framing)."*
3. **Voice:** For your real voice, generate clips muted, then record the lines below (or clone your voice in ElevenLabs) and lay them over the edit. For an AI voice, let Veo speak the lines — keep the same voice setting every clip.
4. **The hook:** you're a modern person in modern clothes reacting to 1975. Keep the modern quarter-zip in every shot — that contrast is the whole gag.

## The narration script (what you say to camera)

Read straight through, this is your voiceover. Each block = one 8-second clip, walking continuously down MG Road.

1. "I just woke up on MG Road in Bangalore — and somehow it's nineteen seventy-five. Look at this. No malls, no honking… just this long, tree-lined boulevard and cool morning air."
2. "This whole stretch is a red-tiled promenade of little shops — tailors, a watch repair, a Bata, the Cauvery emporium. And everyone's just… strolling. Nobody's in a hurry."
3. "Here's Higginbothams. This bookshop's been on this road longer than India's been independent. People browse for an hour and buy one book. That's the pace here."
4. "And the traffic? Watch this. One gentleman on a bicycle. One cream Ambassador. A red double-decker bus. That's it — you could cross this road with your eyes closed."
5. "Okay, this is the one I came for — Lakeview Milk Bar. Fresh ice cream, a proper thick milkshake, about a rupee. This has been the coolest spot in town for decades."
6. "Plaza Theatre — they're showing an English film, hand-painted board ten feet tall, a neat little queue out front. On a Saturday evening, this is where the whole city ends up."
7. "Upstairs there's the Indian Coffee House — waiters in white turbans, twenty-five-paise filter coffee, and old-timers arguing about cricket for hours. Nobody rushes them out."
8. "And it's really the pace that gets me. Cool weather all year, everyone knows everyone, evenings spent walking this boulevard. They call it a pensioner's paradise — I finally get it."
9. "No tech parks, no start-ups, no traffic jams. Just MG Road, an ice cream, and all the time in the world. This is Bangalore before it became Bangalore — and I don't want to go back."
